About Me

I am Brayan S. Zapata-Impata, a PhD student at the University of Alicante (Spain) working in Robotic Grasping and Machine Learning. My research mainly focuses in finding ways to compute grasping points using visual perception as well as checking properties of the grasp using tactile perception. I would like to provide robots with the ability to tackle tasks which are natural for humans like grasping novel objects in the wild, predicting the outcome of a grasp or stabilising a grasped object using multiple robotic fingers.

I am passionate about learning new things and I am continuously looking for challenges. During my free time I love reading books, listening and playing music, and also walking my dog.

Experience

University of Alicante

Robotics Research Engineer

Involved in both research and engineering tasks. I am in charge of designing robotics experiments, developing the entire stack (from hardware to software), evaluating and reporting results. This work is mainly focused on robotic grasping, grasp assessment and control using visual and tactile perception. To achieve this, I am applying learning techniques such as CNNs, LSTMs and GANs in order to provide robots with autonomy and dexterity.

Khoury College of Computer Sciences

Visiting Researcher

Research stay in The Helping Hands Laboratory, head by Dr. Robert Platt, at the Northeastern University (Boston, USA). During my stay, I developed a mobile manipulator system, focusing mainly on providing it with autonomy through the software (task planning, grasping, mobility). I was also in charge of evaluating it on the task of collecting and transporting a variety of objects in a challenging environment outdoors.

Critical Future LTD

Computer Vision Consultant

I led a computer vision project for a healthcare company I was in charge of designing and implementing a working solution for the industry to classify skin moles in order to predict skin cancer using Computer Vision and Deep Learning. This was achieved training with open source databases an ensemble of models, including CNNs in TensorFlow, Gradient Boosting Trees and Support Vector Machines with Scikit-learn.
